## What I'd actually take

These are the ones I hold or have taken myself, not a list pulled from a catalog:

- **[LFCS](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/linux-foundation-certified-sysadmin-lfcs/)** (Linux Foundation Certified System Administrator). The exam is performance based, you work in a real shell against real tasks. If you want a solid Linux foundation for ops, SRE, or backend work, start here. I hold this one.
- **[CKAD](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/certified-kubernetes-application-developer-ckad/)** (Certified Kubernetes Application Developer). Also performance based. If you're a developer deploying to Kubernetes rather than running clusters, this is the right one of the Kubernetes certs. I hold this one too.
- **[LFD121](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/training/developing-secure-software-lfd121/)** (Developing Secure Software). Free course, no excuse not to take it. I took it and still reference it.

If you're an admin or platform engineer rather than a developer, look at the [CKA](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/certified-kubernetes-administrator-cka/) instead of the CKAD.
